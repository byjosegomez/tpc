Frequently Asked Questions

Building a deck or patio cover is a major investment for homeowners in Tacoma and throughout Pierce County. At Tacoma Patio Covers, we know clients often have questions about our services and the materials we use.

Below, we’ve compiled answers to some of the most common FAQs about our deck and patio cover projects. If you don’t see what you’re looking for, feel free to reach out to our team for more details.

  • Permit requirements depend on your city or county and how the patio cover is designed. Details such as size, whether it’s attached to your home, overall height, property setbacks, and total lot coverage can all determine if a permit is required. We help guide homeowners through this process to ensure everything is built to code.

  • Patio covers and pergolas may look similar, but they serve different purposes. A patio cover has a solid roof that keeps rain out for year-round use, while a pergola has an open design that provides shade but doesn’t protect from rain. If you want to stay dry, choose a patio cover. If you just want shade and style, a pergola works great.

  • Most wood patio covers range from $16,000–$20,000 for a 14×14 size (about $80–$102 per square foot), depending on the style.

    Final pricing varies based on size, design, site conditions, and upgrades like tongue-and-groove ceilings, lighting, fans, heaters, and post wraps.

  • It comes down to style and light.

    Wood patio covers offer a warm, traditional look and are ideal if you want to add fans, heaters, lighting, or screens.

    Acrylic patio covers deliver a sleek, modern feel and let natural light pass through—keeping your patio and nearby rooms brighter year-round.

  • Permitting starts with completed drawings delivered within 10 business days of purchase.

    Most cities average about 60 days for approval, though complex lots or busy seasons can extend timelines up to 6 months.

    Starting earlier in the year can help speed things up.

  • We serve Tacoma and the surrounding areas, including Puyallup, Lakewood, University Place, Gig Harbor, Graham, Spanaway, Auburn, and Federal Way.

    Basically, if it’s in Pierce County, we’ve got you covered.

  • A custom patio cover typically delivers a 70–80% return on investment, depending on craftsmanship and long-term care.

    That means you can expect to recoup most of what you invested when it’s time to sell—while enjoying the added comfort and functionality in the meantime.

  • We don’t guess—we inspect.

    If the underlying structure is still solid, replacing a few boards or railings may be all that’s needed.

    But if we find deep rot, unsafe framing, or shifting footings, we’ll explain why a full replacement makes more sense. You’ll always know exactly what we see—and why we recommend it.

  • The Pacific Northwest climate means steady rain, moisture, and long gray seasons—so durability matters.

    We primarily recommend TimberTech composite decking because it resists moisture, rot, and fading while requiring far less maintenance than traditional wood.

    If you prefer real wood, we’ll help you choose the right species and finish to handle Tacoma’s wet conditions and keep it protected for the long haul.

  • We build every project like it’s going in our own backyard.

    That means precise cuts, code-compliant framing, smart layout planning, and clean finishes—right down to the borders on your deck boards.

    We’re not just building for today. We’re building something that feels solid and looks sharp for years to come.

  • Yes, we can handle permits for your deck.

    We create our own plans and submit them to the city—or your HOA if applicable—so you don’t have to worry about the paperwork.

  • We primarily use TimberTech because we believe it’s the best composite decking on the market.

    If you prefer another brand, we’re happy to install it too—our goal is to make sure your deck looks great and lasts for years.

  • We serve Tacoma and the surrounding areas, including Puyallup, Lakewood, University Place, Gig Harbor, Graham, Spanaway, Auburn, and Federal Way.

    Basically, if it’s in Pierce County, we’ve got you covered.

    It provides shade in the summer, shields against rain and snow, and protects your outdoor furniture and finishes.

    Whether entertaining or relaxing, a covered deck or patio is more livable, luxurious, and usable—rain or shine.

  • Absolutely. Ceiling fans, lighting, heaters, and even built-in speakers are popular upgrades for covered decks and patios.
